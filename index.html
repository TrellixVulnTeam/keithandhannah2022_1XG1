Getting Around

Google Maps is your best friend! Many of Chicago's most popular attractions are located downtown, all within walking distance of each other. We recommend downloading Lyft or Uber to easily catch a ride anywhere you need one. Cabs are plentiful in the downtown area and can usually be easily hailed, but will likely be the most expensive option.

Chicago also has a great public transportation system that includes trains and buses across nearly the entire city. You can purchase transit passes online prior to your visit, download the Ventra App, or purchase passes at any CTA train station, and most CVS/Walgreens.

The Bottom Lounge is located conviently near the Ashland Green / Pink line el stop. We encourage guests to use ride share services such as Uber or Lyft to get to and from the venue.

Things To Do

Chicago's official tourism website has tons of helpful information we recommend checking out. If you are visiting Chicago for the first time, downtown is a great place to start exploring!

Millennium Park/Grant Park/The Bean

One of Chicago's most iconic sights, "Cloud Gate", also known as The Bean, is located in Millennium Park. This large park area located right in the loop also connects to Grant Park, another large park and event area. Exploring these areas will allow you to see some great public art, Buckingham Fountain, Maggie Daley Park, Lurie Garden, Pritzker Pavilion, and great skyline views.

Magnificent Mile

Stroll along the Magnificent Mile (and the rest of Michigan Ave) to get a great look into Chicago's architecture and luxury shopping options. As you walk along, you can view some iconic sights, including the Water Tower, Tribune Tower, Marina City Towers, bridges, Riverwalk, Art Institute, Chicago Cultural Center, and interesting city views.

Museum Campus

Located along the Lakefront, Museum Campus is home to great cultural institutions, including the Field Museum, Adler Planetarium, Shedd Aquarium, Soldier Field, and awesome Lakefront/Skyline views.

Architecture

Chicago is well-known for its architectural design styles and inspiring buildings. We highly recommend visiting the Chicago Architecture Foundation to get information and booking one of their many tours to get a great overview of the city sights and history. Their River Cruise is especially popular.

Other Attractions

  • Make time to walk along the beautiful Lakefront Trail
  • Get a bird's eye view of the city from 360 Chicago (John Hancock Building) or Skydeck Chicago (Willis/Sears Tower)
  • Enjoy a meal, have family-friendly fun, view Lake Michigan, and take a ride on the new Centennial Wheel at Navy Pier.
  • Chicago is truly a city of neighborhoods, so once you have seen the sights downtown, get out to one of the 77 distinct neighborhoods. For visitors we recommend: Lincoln Park, Wrigleyville/Southport Corridor, Wicker Park/Bucktown, Chinatown, and the West Loop.
